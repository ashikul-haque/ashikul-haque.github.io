Md Ashikul Haque is a PhD candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Texas at Dallas, advised by Prof. Abusayeed Saifullah. The overarching goal of his research is to make communication for low-power IoT devices more resilient, robust, and secure while minimizing the load on end devices. He builds and evaluates end-to-end systems spanning PHY/MAC mechanisms, signal-level decoding pipelines, and learning-based coordination, validated through SDR testbeds and simulation. His research interests include LPWAN security and reliability, jamming-resilient and coexistence-aware networking, cross-technology communication, and learning-based optimization. His work has been published in ACM/IEEE SenSys (accepted), IEEE INFOCOM, IEEE/ACM MobiHoc, ACM/IEEE IoTDI, IEEE RTSS, ACM EWSN, and SDN-CPS.

Research Interest

Internet of Things; wireless networks; security and robustness; cross-technology communication; and learning-based optimization.
