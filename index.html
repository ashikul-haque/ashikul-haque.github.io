Md Ashikul Haque is a PhD candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Texas at Dallas, advised by Prof. Abusayeed Saifullah. He received a Master’s degree in Computer Science from Wayne State University in 2024. His research interests include Internet of Things, Wireless Networks, and Machine Learning. His work has been published in top conferences, including ACM/IEEE SenSys, IEEE INFOCOM, IEEE RTSS, ACM MobiHoc, ACM/IEEE IoTDI, and ACM EWSN.

Research Interest

The overarching goal of Ashikul’s research is to make communication for low power IoT devices more resilient, robust, and secure, while minimizing the load on end devices. He builds and evaluates end to end systems that span PHY and MAC mechanisms, signal level decoding pipelines, and machine learning based adaptation and coordination. In his work, learning models infer channel and interference conditions, optimize protocol decisions, and enable cross network coexistence with minimal device side overhead. He validates these systems using SDR testbeds and simulation, translating signal and system insights into deployable, data driven designs.
