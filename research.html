My research develops systems, protocols, and learning-based methods for low-power wide-area and wireless IoT networks. I focus on resilient and efficient communication under interference, coexistence, and jamming, spanning anti-jamming defenses for LoRa, learning-based coexistence management, cross-technology communication from LR-FHSS to LoRa, and practical MAC/PHY mechanisms for bursty and time-sensitive traffic. I validate these designs through PyTorch-based learning, NS-3 simulation, SDR prototyping with GNU Radio and USRP, and outdoor or testbed experiments.

Selected Topics
  • Cross-technology communication: signal-level interoperability between LR-FHSS and LoRa, including receiver-side detection and correction.
  • Learning-based coexistence and coordination: deep reinforcement learning and coordinated learning agents for dense LPWAN deployments sharing spectrum.
  • Resilient wireless communication: anti-jamming techniques for single and collaborative attackers in LoRa and LPWAN settings.
  • Practical LPWAN protocol design: measurement-driven MAC/PHY mechanisms for burst traffic, reliability, and time-sensitive wireless or industrial IoT operation.
