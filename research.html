Research

My work spans secure and reliable LPWAN communication, coexistence among heterogeneous networks, and learning-driven optimization under real-world constraints.

Selected research directions

Signal-level cross-technology communication (CTC)

  • Implemented an SDR-based TX/RX pipeline bridging LR-FHSS and LoRa, including receiver-side detection/correction and end-to-end benchmarking.

Learning-based coexistence management

  • Implemented a deep Q-learning coexistence framework in PyTorch and evaluated performance across dense coexistence scenarios using NS-3 and controlled experiments.

Generative coordination (under review)

  • Designed a hypernetwork-based coordination layer that synthesizes mutually exclusive policy updates from model-level parameter exchange among multiple learning-enabled networks.

Jamming defense and robustness

  • Implemented and evaluated receiver/gateway-side recovery and multi-gateway diversity methods under reactive and collaborative jamming.

MAC protocol design for LPWAN

  • Co-designed Burst-MAC for burst traffic with predictable access behavior; evaluated reliability/latency/energy tradeoffs via experiments and simulation.

Tools & platforms
